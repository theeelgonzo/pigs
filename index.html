Raising Ham-izona

Pigs are born in litters average twenty or morebaby piglets. At birth, they are often as long as an adult's forearm and can weigh up to fifteen pounds.

Piglets are curious, but uncoordinated and highly dependent on their mother for the first few weeks of their lives. They subsist almost entirely off of milk from the sow. They are considered weaned at two month's of age. Shortly after this is a great time to procure your first pig!
